📃 Abstract¶

Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) have been widely used for synthesizing new images from various datasets. In this project, we explore the use of GANs for synthesizing new images from the CIFAR-10 dataset. The CIFAR-10 dataset contains 60,000 32x32 color images in 10 classes, which makes it an ideal dataset for exploring the potential of GANs. Our aim is to train a GAN to generate new images that are similar to the ones in the CIFAR-10 dataset, while still being diverse and unique. We compare the results of different GAN architectures, such as DCGAN and WGAN, to determine the best model for generating new images from the CIFAR-10 dataset. Our results demonstrate the potential of GANs in synthesizing high-quality images from the CIFAR-10 dataset, and highlight the importance of choosing the right GAN architecture for the task at hand. This study provides insights into the potential applications of GANs in image synthesis and generation.¶

Loading data¶

Since we will be using an activation layer of Tanh for the last layer of the generator, which maps the data from -1 to 1, The data will be preprocessed by normalizing the images from -1 to 1 using the transforms.Normalize().¶

📊 Class distribution¶

The CIFAR-10 dataset consists of 60,000 32x32 color images in 10 classes, with 6,000 images per class. There are 50,000 training images and 10,000 test images. The classes are equally balanced, with each class containing 6,000 images. The mean pixel value across the train and test set is approximately -0.05 and -0.04. The pixel values range from -1 to 1.¶
